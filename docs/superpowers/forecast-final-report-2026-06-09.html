Can our app predict what Beauty Square will sell?

Final report & recommendation · real sales data Dec 2025 – Jun 2026 · tested 298 product-months · 2026-06-10
The answer in one sentence: Yes — but the simple average wins. The best forecast for a shop with ~6 months of history is the smart run rate the app already has, plus one safety cap. Every fancy model we tested (SARIMA, Croston, XGBoost, calendar boosts) made predictions worse, and one of them once predicted 173,318 units for a product that sold 39.

🧪How we tested (the time machine)

We pretended it was 31 January and let each model see only what it would have known that day. It predicted February. Then we checked February's real sales and scored the miss. Repeat for Feb→Mar, Mar→Apr, Apr→May. No model ever saw the future — exactly how it works in real life.

Score = "typical miss": for the middle-of-the-pack product, how many units off was the monthly prediction? Lower is better. "Crazy forecasts" counts predictions 20× larger than reality.

🔍The three things we learned

1. Simple beat fancy — everywhere. Steady sellers: smart average 8.8 vs SARIMA 36.7. Slow/lumpy sellers: smart average 5.6 vs Croston 8.8. Six months of history simply isn't enough for the clever math to find real patterns — it finds imaginary ones instead.

2. The safety cap is free insurance. Capping every forecast at 3× the product's best-ever month stopped both crazy forecasts completely and never hurt a single sane prediction. There is no reason not to ship this.

3. The holiday/payday boosts are guessing. The current app forecast is the smart average × boost multipliers — and the boosts made it ~45% less accurate, because no Christmas or Valentine's exists in 6 months of data to learn from. Turn them off until the data contains a real holiday season.

📋The recommendation (do this)

  1. Ship the safety cap — every forecast ≤ 3× the product's best trailing month.
  2. New products (<60 days of sales): use the plain 30-day average. (Longer averages divide by days the product didn't exist and guess too low: 9.5 vs 12.3 typical miss.)
  3. Everything else: use the smart average (30/90/365-day blend — already in the app, zero new code).
  4. Turn the holiday/payday boosts off until next January, then re-test them against real December data.
  5. Park SARIMA, Croston and XGBoost — keep the code, don't serve it. They're for customers who arrive with 1+ year of clean history.
  6. Re-run this test every month (it's one command now). The day a fancy model beats the smart average with the cap on — switch. Not before.

⏭️What would make forecasts genuinely better

Not a better model — more history. The single highest-impact action is backfilling the full year of Shopify orders (the read_all_orders scope). That's when Holt-Winters and SARIMA earn a re-trial, and when the holiday boosts can be calibrated on a real Christmas.

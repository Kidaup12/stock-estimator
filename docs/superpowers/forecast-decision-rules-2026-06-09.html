Forecast Retest — Decision Rules

Same walk-forward cases as the first report (Beauty Square, Dec 2025 → Jun 2026), re-scored with simpler run-rates, a 3×-best-month safety cap, and hybrid model policies — sliced by product bucket and history length. Lower typical miss (median units off per product-month) = better.

The decision rules (what the data says to do with ~6 months of history):
  1. Always cap every forecast at 3× the product's best trailing month. Kills all blow-ups at zero accuracy cost. Ship first.
  2. Product has <60 days of history → last-30-day run rate (longer windows divide by days that don't exist and under-forecast).
  3. Product has 60+ days → weighted run rate (30/90/365 blend) — won every bucket, steady and lumpy. Already in the codebase.
  4. Drop SARIMA now (worst + only blow-up source). Croston/TSB too — loses to the weighted rate on its own lumpy bucket. Shelved, not serving.
  5. Calendar boosts off until a holiday season exists in the data; re-test per-signal then.
  6. SES tested, not adopted: loses to the weighted rate on every clean month (Jan/Feb/Mar); its apparent overall win comes entirely from the gap-affected Apr→May round. Re-test after the backfill repairs May.
  7. XGBoost stays shelved (no out-of-sample benefit, round 1).
  8. Re-run this backtest monthly; flip models only when one beats the weighted rate with the cap on.

Honesty note: Apr→May biases are over-stated (May sales data thin — sync gap). Rankings hold.
